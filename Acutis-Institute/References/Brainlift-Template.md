## How to Use This Template

A **Brainlift** is the canonical Alpha School / Trilogy / Superbuilders artifact for demonstrating that you have built *genuine domain expertise* — deep enough to develop your own contrarian, defensible takes. It is structured around Norman Webb's **Depth of Knowledge (DOK)** taxonomy, inverted from highest to lowest signal:

| Section | DOK Level | What it shows |
|---|---|---|
| Spiky Points of View | **DOK 4 — Extended Thinking** | Non-consensus, original takes. *Proof of expertise.* |
| Insights | **DOK 3 — Strategic Thinking** | Synthesized abstractions from research. *Proof of judgment.* |
| Experts | — | Who shaped your thinking. *Proof of intellectual lineage.* |
| Knowledge Tree | **DOK 2 + DOK 1** | Facts and summaries. *Proof of work — the audit trail.* |

**The Brainlift is read top-down for signal, bottom-up for provenance.** The reader sees your contrarian takes immediately; if they want to interrogate them, every claim traces back through Insights → Knowledge Tree to a primary source.

### Three principles before you start

1. **This is a living document.** Update it weekly as you learn. A static Brainlift is a dead Brainlift. *(Austin Scholar #174)*
2. **Only fabricate nothing.** Experts cited must have actually shaped your thinking. Facts cited must be verifiable in the original sources. The Trilogy BrainLift CLI explicitly rejects "filling every template section" — *empty is better than fake.*
3. **The chain must be auditable.** Every SPOV traces to ≥2 Insights. Every Insight traces to ≥2 Knowledge Tree nodes. If removing two random nodes wouldn't weaken any SPOV, those nodes are decorative — cut them.

### Filling-in convention

- `{{Placeholder}}` — replace with your content
- `> *Guidance: ...*` — italicized prompts that explain what good looks like. **Delete these as you fill the section.**
- `[strikethrough or omit]` — sections you may skip if genuinely empty. Don't pad.

### Target output: a Brainlift that gets you hired

This template is calibrated to the **Superbuilders / Gauntlet AI / Alpha School hiring bar**:
- Patrick Skinner's stated cadence: **1–2 new apps piloted every week.** Solutions must be shippable at this tempo.
- Austen Allred's stated Gauntlet bar: **98th-percentile intelligence, 95th-percentile coding ability.**
- Gauntlet values: **Extreme Ownership, Accuracy Over Everything, Meritocracy Wins, Relentless Learning.**

A Brainlift that doesn't pass the **10-point hiring self-audit** at the bottom of this template is not ready to send.

## DOK 4 — Spiky Points of View

> *"Spiky points of view are strong opinions, loosely held. They start a conversation, they don't end it."*
> — Wes Kao

> *Guidance: This is the most important section. **Five SPOVs is the canonical count** — fewer feels thin, more dilutes. Each SPOV must pass Wes Kao's **4 Ps**:*
>
> 1. **Personal** — Grounded in your authentic experience. Answer "why does YOU of all people see this?"
> 2. **Particular** — A smart insider could argue the opposite with conviction. If everyone agrees, it's not spiky.
> 3. **Provocative** — Surprises by reframing or refuting an accepted idea.
> 4. **Purposeful** — Serves the reader's best interests. Not contrarian for sport.
>
> *Common failure modes (delete the SPOV if any of these are true):*
> - *Dressed-up consensus — the SPOV everyone already nods at.*
> - *"Yes-and" SPOV — adds nuance instead of taking a stance.*
> - *Disconnected from your build — interesting take that nothing follows from.*
> - *Solution is hand-wavy — couldn't ship it in 2 weeks.*

## Hiring-Grade Self-Audit

> *Guidance: Before submitting, you should be able to answer YES to all 10 questions below. Any NO is a known weakness — fix it or be ready to defend the gap.*

- [ ] **1. SPOVs survive a smart insider's attack.** Could a senior person at Alpha / Superbuilders argue against each of my 5 SPOVs *with conviction*? (If no, they're consensus dressed as contrarian.)
- [ ] **2. The chain is auditable.** Does each SPOV trace cleanly to ≥2 DOK 3 Insights, and does each Insight trace to ≥2 Knowledge Tree nodes?
- [ ] **3. Solutions are shippable in 2 weeks.** Could I start building each Solution Monday morning and have something demonstrable by the end of week 2? (The Superbuilders cadence is 1–2 apps/week.)
- [ ] **4. Canonical voices are cited by name.** Have I cited Liemandt, Mackenzie Price, Wes Kao, Norman Webb, and Mollick by name in either Experts or Knowledge Tree? Missing any of these is a tell.
- [ ] **5. Primary learning-science sources are present.** Have I cited at least 3 of: Bloom (1984, 2-Sigma Problem), Hattie (formative feedback meta-analysis), Sweller (Cognitive Load Theory), Deci & Ryan (Self-Determination Theory), Vygotsky (Zone of Proximal Development)?
- [ ] **6. Motivation is treated as the primary variable.** At least one SPOV directly engages with motivation, not just tech/AI/curriculum. (Liemandt: "Motivation is 90% of the solution.")
- [ ] **7. Voice is insider, not observer.** Does this Brainlift sound like I'm already in the room ("we," specific app names, real product references), or like a third-party analyst?
- [ ] **8. Experts are a balanced portfolio.** Mix of researchers, builders, and operators — not just influencers or just academics.
- [ ] **9. Out-of-Scope forecloses follow-ups.** Is the Out-of-Scope list specific enough that no stakeholder will ask "wait, but what about X?" because X is already on the list?
- [ ] **10. Every citation is defensible under interrogation.** Could I be asked about any single source and explain what's in it, where I read it, and why it matters? (If no, remove the citation — fakes get caught.)

> *Guidance: Score honestly. 10/10 → submit. 8–9/10 → fix the gaps, then submit. ≤7/10 → not ready.*

## Living Document Discipline

> *Guidance: A Brainlift is not a deliverable — it's a working artifact. Discipline for keeping it alive:*

1. **Weekly insight pass.** Each week, add ≥1 new DOK 1 fact, ≥1 new DOK 2 summary, ≥1 new piece of evidence supporting or challenging an existing SPOV.
2. **SPOV stress-test cadence.** Monthly, attempt to *kill* one of your own SPOVs. If you can't kill it, it gets stronger. If you can, replace it.
3. **Tool stack:** Per Austin Scholar's workflow — feed your DOK 3 Insights into a reasoning model (OpenAI o-3 or equivalent), ask for deep research, sort outputs into DOK 1 and DOK 2 nodes. Iterate. Optionally upload to EPHOR for a personalized learning feed, or use Brainmaxxing for daily expert content keyed to your Brainlift.
4. **Version control.** Track changes. The diff over time tells the story of your expertise compounding.
