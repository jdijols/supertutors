# Assumptions: A Critique

A critique of [**"We're Teaching Our Kids to Succeed in a World That Will Soon No Longer Exist"**](https://notes.nessielabs.com/share/2576cf1a-f653-49f1-a985-61791187a8b9/).

Each section below pairs an assumption from the article with a counter-position (SPOV) grounded in the classical and Christian tradition.

---

## 1. On the Purpose of Education

> **Article's Assumption:** The purpose of education is to become and remain economically valuable in a dynamic job market.
>
> "If we want to prepare our children for this reality, we need to fundamentally rethink what education is for. It's not for transmitting a fixed body of knowledge. It's for developing the capacity to learn, unlearn, and relearn continuously throughout a lifetime. It's for building Human Skill Adaptability Rate (HSAR)."

### SPOV 1 — The purpose of education is the cultivation of wisdom and virtue.

- **Plato**, *The Republic* ([518b–d](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus:text:1999.01.0168:book%3D7:page%3D518))
  > "Education is the art of directing the soul's focus toward the Good, not just acquiring information."

- **Aristotle**, *Nicomachean Ethics* ([1104b](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus:text:1999.01.0054:bekker+page%3D1104b))
  > "Education is about training oneself to feel pleasure and pain in the right things."

---

## 2. On What Determines Success

> **Article's Assumption:** Skill adaptability is the most crucial component for success today and into the future.
>
> "We measure success by test scores in Math, Reading, Writing, History, and Science. These are important skills, and I'm not suggesting we abandon them. But we are missing something far more crucial, something that will determine whether our children thrive or struggle in the world they're about to inherit. We are not teaching our children how to adapt."

### SPOV 2 — Success is not best measured by socio-economic status, but by achieving salvation in the world to come.

- **Socrates** (via Plato), *Apology* ([30b](https://sententiaeantiquae.com/2014/12/10/plato-apology-30b-socrates-words-on-virtue-and-wealth/))
  > "I tell you that virtue does not come from money, but from virtue comes money and every other good of man, public as well as private."

- **Aristotle**, *Nicomachean Ethics* ([1096a](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus:text:1999.01.0054:bekker+page%3D1096a))
  > "Wealth is evidently not the good we are seeking; for it is merely useful and for the sake of something else."

- **Saint Paul**, *2 Timothy 4:7* ([KJV](https://www.kingjamesbibleonline.org/2-Timothy-4-7/))
  > "I have fought a good fight, I have finished my course, I have kept the faith."

---

## 3. On the Core Problem to Solve

> **Article's Assumption:** The unsolved problem is measuring and developing adaptability in students.
>
> "Here's the honest truth: we don't yet know how to measure HSAR. We don't know how to develop it systematically in every student. We don't know how to scale approaches that work in one school to thousands of schools. We don't know how to account for cultural differences in how people learn and adapt. We don't know how to ensure that HSAR development is equitable, reaching students from all backgrounds and circumstances."

### SPOV 3 — The real challenge is applying unchanging principles to a rapidly changing environment.

How do we navigate disruption while staying anchored to core values? The split is between the **timeless** (the *why* and *who*) and the **temporary** (the *what* and *how*).

- **Saint Augustine**, *On True Religion* ([39.72](https://archive.org/details/st.augustineoftruerelilgion))
  > "Do not go abroad. Return within yourself. In the inward man dwells truth. If you find that you are by nature mutable, transcend yourself."

- **Saint Thomas Aquinas**, *Summa Theologica* ([II-II, Q. 47](https://www.newadvent.org/summa/3047.htm))
  > "Prudence applies universal principles to the particular conclusions of practical matters... it belongs to prudence to apply right reason to things that can happen in different ways."

---

## 4. On What Makes a Meaningful Life

> **Article's Assumption:** A meaningful life is marked by career advancement.
>
> "In other words, your value in the job market, your ability to thrive in your career, your capacity to create a meaningful life depends on whether your HSAR is greater than the SRR. If you're adapting faster than skills are becoming obsolete, you're winning. If you're adapting slower than skills are becoming obsolete, you're losing."

### SPOV 4 — A meaningful life is marked by self-sacrifice and the sincere attempt to know, love, and serve God.

- **Saint Augustine**, *Confessions* ([1.1.1](https://www.thelatinlibrary.com/augustine/conf1.shtml))
  > "You have made us for yourself, O Lord, and our hearts are restless until they find their rest in you."
